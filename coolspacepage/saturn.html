Planet Saturn

Sixth from the sun is Saturn, my personal favorite planet in our solar system. Like Jupiter, it is one of the gas giants in our Solar System. Saturn gets its namesake from being the Roman Father of Jupiter. Saturn has a distance of 9.5 astronomical units (one astronomical unit is the distance between the Sun and Earth) from the Sun, taking 29.45 Earth years to complete it's orbit. It takes light 82 minutes to reach Saturn from the Sun! Saturn has a radius of 58,232 kilometers (36,183.7 miles), a mass of 5.68 x 10²⁶ kilograms, and a volume 8.27 x 10¹⁴ cubic kilometers. Its surface temperature is -176 degrees Celsius (-285 degrees Fahrenheit). Saturn is most recognizable for it's rings, made of pieces of comets, asteroids, and bits of shattered moons that broke up before they reached the planet. The ring system extends up to 282,000 kilometers (175,000 miles) from the planet, but the rings are only as tall as 30 feet (10 meters). Saturn has 62 moons, making it the planet with the second most moons.
