Planet Neptune

Neptune, being named after the Roman God of the Sea, is the eigth (and final) planet from the sun in our solar system. Like Uranus, it's an ice giant composed of mostly a hot, dense fluid of "icy" materials, like water, methane and ammonia. Neptune is roughly 30 astronomical units away from the sun, taking 164.8 Earth years to complete its orbit. It takes light 249 minutes to reach Neptune from the Sun, with each Neptunian day lasting 16 hours. Neptune has 14 moons. Neptune's radius is 24,622 kilometers (15,299.4 miles), a mass of 1.024 x 10²⁶ kilograms, and a volume of 6.254 x 10¹³ cubic kilometers. The surface temperature of Neptune is -225 degrees Celsius (-373 degrees Fahrenheit and 48 degrees Kelvin), with wind speeds of 2,000 kilometers per hour (1,200 miles per hour).
