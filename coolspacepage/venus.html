Planet Venus

Venus may be the second closest terrestrial planet to the sun, but it's the hottest planet in our solar system! At approximately 108,562,550 KM (67,457,600 Miles), it takes light just over 6 minutes to reach this planet. Venus has a volume of 9.2841 x 10¹¹km³, and a mass of 4.8673 x 10²⁴kg. Named after the Roman goddess of love, Venus traps in the heat from the sun with it's thick, toxic atmosphere and is smothered in thick clouds of sulfuric acid, with surface temperatures reaching 475 degrees Celsius (900 degrees Fahrenheit). That's hot enough to melt lead! It takes Venus roughly 225 Earth days to complete an orbit around the sun, but it takes 243 Earth days to complete a rotation. That means a Venusian day lasts longer than its year!
