Planet Jupiter

Jupiter is the fifth planet from the sun, getting it's namesake from the Roman King of Gods. Unlike the four planets before it, Jupiter is a gas giant, the biggest planet of our solar system with a whopping diameter of 142,984 kilometers (88,846 Miles), a volume of 1,321 Earths, and a mass of 318 Earths. Jupiter is 5.1 astronomical units (one astronomical unit is the distance between the Sun and Earth) away from the Sun, with an orbit taking 11.86 years to complete. On the other hand, one day on Jupiter is just 10 hours long! It takes light 41.2 minutes to get from the sun to Jupiter. It's surface temperature is -110 degrees Celsius (-160 degrees Fahrenheit). It is the first planet from the sun to have rings, and has 79 moons, making it the planet with the most moons in our solar system. Jupiter is most famously known for the Great Red Spot on it's southern hemisphere. The storm itself is twice as wide as Earth!
