Planet Uranus

Uranus is the seventh planet from the sun, and is named after the Greek God of the Sky. Uranus is an ice giant, but most of its mass is a hot, dense fluid of "icy" materials, such as water, methane and ammonia. Uranus is 19.8 astronomical units from the sun, it takes light 164 minutes to reach Uranus from the sun, and Uranus makes an orbit around the Sun every 84 Earth years. A day on Uranus is 17 hours and 14 minutes, but what's interesting about Uranus is that it rotates on its side with an axis of rotation with 60 degrees. One side of Uranus will always face the sun, and the other side will always face away from the sun. Uranus has a radius of 25,362 kilometers (15,759.2 miles), a mass of 8.6810 x 10²⁵ kilograms, and a volume of 6.83 x 10¹³ cubic kilometers. Uranus has a minimum temperature of 49 degrees Kelvin (-224.2 degrees Celsius), with winds that can reach up to 900 kilometers per hour (560 miles per hour). Uranus has 27 moons.
