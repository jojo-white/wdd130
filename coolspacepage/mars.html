Planet Mars

Mars is the fourth terrestrial planet from the sun. We have sent many rovers to mars to explore it's surface, since it's the closest planet to Earth that won't melt whatever we send to it within the hour (thanks, Venus). Mars is approximately 225,568,600 KM (140,161,800 Miles) from the sun. It is a cold, dusty planet with a very thin atmosphere, with polar ice caps, canyons, and even some volcanos that have gone extinct. It has the largest mountain in the solar system, Olympus Mons, at a height of 72,000 feet. A day on Mars takes 24.6 hours, and a year on Mars is 669.6 sols (or 687 Earth days). Mars has a radius of 3,390 Kilometers (2,106 miles), a mass of 0.64169 x 10²⁴ kilograms, and a volume of 16.312 x 10¹⁰ cubic kilometers. Mars has two moons, Phobos and Deimos, and is named after the Roman god of war.
