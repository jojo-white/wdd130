Dwarf Planet Pluto

Last, but definitely not least, is the dwarf planet Pluto. Pluto is ninth from the sun, named after the Roman god of the Underworld. At a distance of 39 astronomical units, Pluto takes 249 Earth years to complete its orbit around the sun. It takes light 5.5 hours to reach Pluto from the sun, and a day on Pluto is 153 hours. It's classified as a dwarf planet, with a radius of 1,151 kilometers (715 miles), a mass of 1.309 x 10²², and a volume of 6.4 billion cubic kilometers. Pluto's surface temperature is -232 degrees Celsius (387 degrees Fahrenheit). Pluto has a region on it's surface called Sputnik Planitia, etched by fields of small pits that might've been formed by a process called sublimation, where ice turns directly from solid to gas. It looks like a heart, as seen on the right side of the planet in the picture! Pluto has five moons.
