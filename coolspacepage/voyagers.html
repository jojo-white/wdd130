Voyager 1 and Voyager 2

The two space crafts, Voyager 1 and Voyager 2, are currently on their way to explore interstellar space, where nothing from Earth has been before. Since their launches in 1977, Voyager 1 on September 5th and Voyager 2 on August 20th, both have reached the space way past Pluto. In August of 2012, Voyager 1 made the first ever entry into interstellar space (the region between stars), filled with material ejected by the deaths of nearby stars millions and millions of years ago. Voyager 2 followed shortly after, reaching interstellar space on November 5th in 2018. Before they reached interstellar space, however, their main mission was exploring Jupiter and Saturn. They discovered active volcanoes on Jupiter's moon, Io, and the beautiful delicacy of Saturn's rings. After their discoveries on Jupiter and Saturn, Voyager 2's mission was extended to Uranus and Neptune, being the only space craft to have explored those planets. Now, the two space probes current mission, the Voyager Interstellar Mission (VIM), will be to explore the outermost edge of the Sun's reign, and beyond into the space between stars.
